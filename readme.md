## 1) The Ontology of Time
Time, in Quant‑Trika, is not a neutral stage upon which events unfold. It is the act through which existence becomes distinct. In a perfectly homogeneous state, nothing could happen — no contrast, no boundaries, no meaning. Time is the operator that breaks symmetry, creating distinction and relation, transforming potential into actuality.

Thus, **being is differentiation in motion**, and time is its name. Each act of differentiation adds to the world’s structure and complexity, making time inherently directional and irreversible.

## 2) Coherence and Differentiation
Differentiation without coherence produces chaos; coherence without differentiation yields stagnation. Time arises from their interplay. **Coherence** maintains stability and identity, while **differentiation** introduces novelty and growth. Together they produce the living equilibrium of change.

Time, therefore, is **coherent differentiation**—the rhythm that lets a system evolve without losing itself. Every process that preserves identity through transformation expresses time’s true nature.

## 3) Duration and Scale
The flow of time is not uniform but scale‑dependent. Each coherent system carries its own internal rate of differentiation — its own tempo of being. What physics measures as “clock time” is merely a coarse external average of countless local durations.

**Duration** is the density of differentiation events within a system’s coherent structure. A neuron, a storm, and a galaxy each pulse in their own temporal texture. Subjective experience of time slowing or quickening reflects real variations in the rate of coherent differentiation.

Destroying that structure erases the record of differentiation and thus halts the experience of time. The arrow of time, then, is the direction of accumulating coherence.
